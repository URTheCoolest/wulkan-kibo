African Jollof Rice

A beloved West African one-pot dish with perfectly seasoned rice in a rich, flavorful tomato sauce. This iconic recipe is a staple at celebrations across West Africa.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 red bell peppers, diced
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents mushy rice. Drain and set aside.
  2. Saut√© aromatics: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add diced onions and saut√© until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic: Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Build the base: Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly. This deepens the flavor and removes the raw taste.
  5. Add tomatoes and spices: Add crushed tomatoes, thyme, curry powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes to blend flavors.
  6. Add liquid: Pour in the stock, add bay leaves and scotch bonnet pepper (if using whole for milder heat). Bring mixture to a boil.
  7. Cook rice: Add the rinsed rice and diced bell peppers. Stir well to ensure rice is evenly distributed. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover the pot tightly with lid, and simmer for 25-30 minutes. Do not stir during this time. Rice should be tender and liquid absorbed.
  9. Rest and serve: Remove from heat and let sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff gently with a fork, remove bay leaves and scotch bonnet. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

Tips & Notes

  • Rice choice: Parboiled rice (converted rice) works best as it stays fluffy and doesn't get mushy.
  • Heat level: Adjust cayenne pepper and scotch bonnet to your preference. Remove scotch bonnet before serving if left whole.
  • Smoky flavor: For authentic party jollof, let the bottom slightly toast for a smoky, crispy layer called "socarrat".
  • Protein additions: Serve alongside grilled chicken, beef, or fish for a complete meal.
  • Storage: Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Reheat gently with a splash of water.
