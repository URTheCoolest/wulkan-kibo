West African Peanut Stew

A creamy, comforting vegetarian stew that combines the rich nuttiness of peanut butter with sweet potatoes and hearty greens. This dish is popular across West Africa and is naturally vegan-friendly.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 3/4 cup natural peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cups fresh spinach or kale, roughly chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped peanuts for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Saut√© aromatics: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add diced onion and saut√© until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger: Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly until fragrant.
  3. Build flavor base: Stir in diced tomatoes with their juices, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 3 minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  4. Create peanut sauce: Add peanut butter and vegetable broth to the pot. Whisk vigorously until peanut butter is fully incorporated and the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  5. Cook sweet potatoes: Add cubed sweet potatoes to the stew. Bring mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sweet potatoes are fork-tender.
  6. Add greens: Stir in fresh spinach or kale and cook for 2-3 minutes until wilted. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Serve: Ladle hot stew into bowls. Garnish with chopped peanuts and fresh cilantro. Serve with steamed rice, quinoa, or crusty bread.

Tips & Notes

  • Peanut butter: Use natural peanut butter without added sugar for authentic flavor. Almond butter can be substituted for nut allergies.
  • Protein boost: Add chickpeas, black-eyed peas, or cubed tofu for extra protein.
  • Consistency: If stew is too thick, add more broth. If too thin, simmer uncovered to reduce.
  • Make ahead: This stew tastes even better the next day. Store in refrigerator for up to 5 days or freeze for up to 3 months.
  • Spice level: Adjust cayenne pepper to your preference. Add a squeeze of lime juice for brightness.
