Plantain Fritters (Kelewele)

Kelewele is a beloved Ghanaian street food featuring ripe plantains seasoned with aromatic spices and fried to crispy perfection. The combination of sweet plantains and spicy seasonings creates an irresistible snack.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe plantains (yellow with black spots for best sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust for heat preference)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying (about 3-4 cups)

Instructions

  1. Prepare plantains: Peel the ripe plantains. Cut them into bite-sized cubes (about 1-inch) or diagonal slices. The pieces should be uniform for even cooking.
  2. Make spice mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine grated ginger, finely chopped onion, cayenne pepper, paprika, ground cloves, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to create an aromatic spice blend.
  3. Marinate plantains: Add the cut plantain pieces to the spice mixture. Gently toss with your hands or a spoon to coat each piece evenly with the seasonings. Let marinate for 10 minutes to absorb flavors.
  4. Heat oil: Pour vegetable oil into a deep pan, skillet, or pot until about 2 inches deep. Heat over medium-high heat to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Test readiness by dropping in a small piece of plantain - it should sizzle immediately.
  5. Fry in batches: Carefully add plantain pieces to the hot oil in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for 3-4 minutes per side, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  6. Drain and serve: Remove fried plantains with a slotted spoon and transfer to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve immediately while hot and crispy. Enjoy as a snack or side dish!

Tips & Notes

  • Plantain ripeness: Use ripe plantains with yellow skin and black spots for the perfect balance of sweetness. Avoid green plantains - they're too starchy for this recipe.
  • Oil temperature: Maintaining the right oil temperature (350¬∞F) is crucial. Too hot and they'll burn outside while staying raw inside; too cool and they'll absorb too much oil.
  • Spice variations: Adjust cayenne pepper based on your heat tolerance. Some recipes include nutmeg or anise for additional flavor complexity.
  • Serving suggestions: Kelewele is traditionally served as a snack with peanuts or as a side dish with grilled fish or chicken. Also great for breakfast!
  • Storage: Best enjoyed fresh, but leftovers can be refrigerated for 2 days and reheated in an oven at 350¬∞F for 10 minutes to restore crispness.
